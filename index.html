About us

Trek Maharashtra is your gateway to exploring the untouched beauty of Maharashtra’s vast and rugged landscapes. From the towering peaks of the Western Ghats to the tranquil valleys of the Sahyadri ranges, we offer an array of trekking adventures designed for both nature enthusiasts and adrenaline seekers. Devkund Waterfall Trek:

Located near Bhira in Maharashtra, Devkund is a beautiful waterfall surrounded by lush forests. It's a relatively easy trek, making it ideal for beginners. The waterfall is especially stunning during the monsoon season, attracting nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Rajgad Fort Trek:

Rajgad, meaning "King of Forts," was once the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Located near Pune, this trek offers a mix of history and adventure. The fort is famous for its grand structure and panoramic views of the Sahyadri range. It’s a moderate trek, great for those interested in historical treks.

Harishchandragad Trek:

Harishchandragad is one of the most famous and challenging treks in the Western Ghats, located in the Ahmednagar district. It offers breathtaking views, especially from Kokankada, a sheer cliff that gives an incredible panoramic view of the Konkan region. The fort is known for its ancient caves, temples, and the mesmerizing Taramati Peak, which is one of the highest points in Maharashtra. The trek is moderately difficult and attracts adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. It's particularly popular among trekkers who enjoy overnight camping.

Sindhudurg Fort:

Located on the Malvan coast in the Konkan region, Sindhudurg Fort is a sea fort built by Shivaji Maharaj. Though it's not a typical mountain trek, reaching the fort involves a boat ride, and exploring it feels like a journey through history. The fort offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea and is known for its historical importance.
